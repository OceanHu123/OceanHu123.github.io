Sushi

Sushi is a Japanese dish made from vinegared rice seasoned with vinegar, sugar, and salt, made from small, slightly sweet Japanese pearl rice, cooled and topped with fish, seafood, vegetables, eggs, or other meats. Sushi can be enjoyed as both a snack and a meal, and in addition to being a favorite food of Japanese people, it is also popular outside of Japan, and there are countless sushi restaurants around the world.


Different types of sushi

sushi roll

  1. A sushi roll, also known as makizushi, is a popular type of sushi that consists of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients and rolled inside a sheet of nori (seaweed). The roll is typically cylindrical in shape and is often cut into bite-sized pieces before serving.

hand-rolled sushi

  1. Hand-rolled sushi, known as temaki in Japanese, is a style of sushi where the ingredients are rolled by hand using a sheet of nori (seaweed) to create a cone or cylindrical shape. Unlike other types of sushi rolls that are typically made using a bamboo mat (makisu), temaki is rolled directly by hand, allowing for a more free-form and casual presentation.

Riba Sushi

  1. There is no specific type of sushi called "Riba sushi" in traditional Japanese sushi terminology or commonly known sushi varieties. Sushi typically consists of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients like raw fish, seafood, vegetables, and occasionally other elements like sauces or condiments

Gunkan Sushi

  1. Gunkan sushi, also known as "gunkan-maki," is a type of sushi that consists of a small oblong-shaped mound of sushi rice wrapped with a strip of nori (seaweed) around its perimeter, forming a "battleship" or "warship" shape. The seaweed strip is taller than the rice and acts as a vessel to hold various toppings or fillings that might be difficult to place directly on the rice due to their texture or consistency.

What is Sushi?

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw fish (sashimi), vegetables, and nori (seaweed), often served with pickled ginger, wasabi, and soy sauce.

How to Eat Sushi

When eating sushi:

  1. Use chopsticks or fingers (for certain types).
  2. Dip the sushi lightly into soy sauce.
  3. Avoid putting too much wasabi into soy sauce.
  4. Enjoy it in one bite if possible.
